The initial decision revolves around the evil dragon's heart:

| Choice Category | Specific Choice                      | Outcome Type | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |

|                 | Interact with the Little Dragon (Hidden) | Good         | During the scene where you decide on the heart, locate and interact with Mini Durin in the top-left corner of the storybook. | **Bad Endings** | Destroy the evil dragon's heart      | Bad          | If you choose to destroy the heart, Albedo will be unable to create an artificial body for Mini Durin, leading to a negative resolution for the quest. Following the decision regarding the evil dragon's heart, if you chose a good path, you'll face another set of choices related to holding back the darkness.

| Choice Category | Specific Choice           | Outcome Type | Description                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :-------------- | :------------------------ | :----------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Good Ending** | Hold back the darkness    | Good         | This is the only good choice in this sequence. Successfully holding back the darkness allows you to complete the fifth part of the quest, securing Durin's future.                 |
| **Bad Endings** | Give in / Run out of time | Bad          | You have 59 seconds to make a choice. Failing to select "Hold back the darkness" or running out of time results in Durin being consumed by darkness, leading to a tragic end for his story. |
|                 | Return to the castle in Simulanka | Bad          | This choice mirrors the bad ending from Part 4, with Durin retreating to Simulanka and his story fading away. It represents an avoidance of his fate rather than confronting it.        |
