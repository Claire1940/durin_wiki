"Fate's Accursed Wiles" is the fifth part of Durin's Story Quest, Draco Rubedo Chapter: Act I - "The Magic Known as Storytelling." This segment guides the Traveler and Durin through a series of introspective and action-packed events, ultimately leading to a critical decision.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Day 5

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Rendezvous in Mondstadt">
    Begin by meeting Durin in Mondstadt City, near the Adventurer's Guild. You'll find him engaged in a game of Genius Invokation TCG with Kaeya, Klee, and Fischl. Durin expresses his desire to make more friends and learn human socialization.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Journey to Stormterror's Lair">
    Follow Durin to Stormterror's Lair. The narrative suggests this is a place where dragons live, hinting at a connection to Dvalin. Entering this area will transport you back into the story world.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter the Story World and Castle">
    Inside the story world, you'll encounter a castle that represents Durin's loneliness. Your goal is to find a way into this castle, specifically the tall tower at its center where the "little dragon" (a younger version of Durin) is hiding.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Traverse the Castle's Barriers">
    Use a magical telescope to manipulate blocks and overcome obstacles within the castle. You may need to find additional blocks to progress. Along the way, you'll encounter toy-like representations of companions from Simulanka (Nilou, Navia, Kirara) who are also trying to reach the little dragon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Confront the Little Dragon">
    Reach the little dragon in the tower. Durin will speak to his past self, urging him to accept friendship and overcome his fear of his "claws and scales." Alice, in her "merry-witch" form, will appear, offering a blessing to grant the little dragon's wish to be free from his curse.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="The Critical Choice">
    After leaving the story world, you'll meet Albedo on Dragonspine. He explains that Durin's connection to his will and body is fading, and the Abyssal power could take over. You will be presented with a choice inside the story world concerning the evil dragon's heart:
    - **Destroy it:** Attempt to eradicate the heart, which might lead to Durin fading away.
    - **Fuse with it:** Accept the heart and the power within, with the potential to rewrite fate.
    - **Return to the castle:** Avoid the choice and retreat, essentially ignoring the problem.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
Choosing to destroy the evil dragon's heart can lead to a "bad ending" where Durin's story fades into nothingness. Be mindful of your decision, as it has significant narrative consequences.
</Callout>

### Understanding the Choices

| Choice Option           | Immediate Outcome                                                                                                | Potential Consequences                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| :---------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Destroy the Heart**   | The little dragon attempts to destroy the evil dragon's heart with friends' help.                                | The evil dragon's heart is like a tree with deep roots; cutting branches (destroying it) doesn't kill it, but makes new growth impossible. Durin's story fades, leaving only the evil dragon's tale. This is a **bad ending** where Durin is forgotten.                                                                                                                   |
| **Fuse with the Heart** | The little dragon accepts the evil dragon's heart, leading to a new life.                                        | The evil dragon's heart beats in his chest, guided by the little dragon's soul. Fate is rewritten, and the little dragon is "born anew." This path leads to a **happy ending** where Durin learns to control the Abyssal power and embrace his new identity, continuing his story with friends.                                                                         |
| **Return to the Castle** | The little dragon hides within the castle walls, avoiding the problem.                                           | This leads to an ending where Durin feels weak and his promise to bring back memories is unfulfilled. It's another **bad ending** where he remains trapped by his fears and the problem is left unresolved, akin to pretending nothing ever happened.                                                                                                                            |
| **Suppress Abyssal Power** | An attempt to suppress the Abyssal power without enough "ink" (story progression) to write it in. | The story gets stuck, indicating that suppressing the power requires more narrative development. This is another **bad ending** that emphasizes the need for Durin to actively write his own fate. |
| **Go to Unwritten Chapter** | Durin decides to explore the "blank pages of the unwritten chapter ahead," seeking a new future. | This is the path that leads to the true happy ending. With the help of his friends and Alice's magic, Durin learns to write his own story, accepting the Abyssal power and transforming it.
