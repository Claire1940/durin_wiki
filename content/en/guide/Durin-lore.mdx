<Section icon={Map} title="The Origins of Durin: An Abyssal Creation">
Durin, the colossal Abyssal dragon whose remains now form the chilling landscape of Dragonspine, has a deeply intertwined and tragic history within Teyvat. Created by the alchemist Rhinedottir, also known as "Gold," Durin was a product of the Art of Khemia from Khaenri'ah. Despite his destructive actions, his true nature was far from malicious.

**Video Highlights:**
- Durin's story is primarily told through limited-time events in Genshin Impact.
- He was created by Rhinedottir and caused havoc during the Cataclysm.
- Venti and Dvalin fought Durin, with Dvalin delivering the final blow.
- Durin's blood contains highly corruptive Abyssal power.
- He harbored no ill will towards his adversaries, wishing for a different encounter.

Rhinedottir considered Durin one of her "miraculous creations," alongside Albedo. However, she noted that Durin lacked the "will" to fully control his immense strength, and the Abyssal power inherent in his being twisted his fate from the beginning. Before Durin's invasion of Teyvat, Rhinedottir had also initiated the Primordial Human Project, and her first attempt, Subject Two, was deemed a failure and left inside Durin. This act would have unforeseen consequences for the dragon.

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Cataclysm and the Fall of Durin">
Five hundred years ago, during the devastating Cataclysm, Durin emerged in Mondstadt, causing widespread destruction. At the time, the Knights of Favonius were severely weakened from their campaign in Khaenri'ah, leaving Mondstadt vulnerable. Hearing the desperate pleas of his people, Barbatos (Venti) awoke and summoned Dvalin to defend the nation.

Barbatos and Dvalin engaged Durin in a fierce battle, luring him over the snowy peaks of Vindagnyr. Dvalin ultimately delivered the killing blow by piercing Durin's neck, sending the colossal dragon plummeting to the area now known as Wyrmrest Valley. This event forever changed the landscape, transforming Vindagnyr into the frigid Dragonspine we know today.

The aftermath of Durin's fall had lasting repercussions. His highly toxic, Abyssal blood soaked into Dragonspine's Ley Lines, inadvertently reviving the Frostbearing Tree, which had been destroyed by Celestia's Skyfrost Nail centuries prior. This interaction also led to the formation of Crimson Agate and Scarlet Quartz, both imbued with Durin's life energy and blood toxins.

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Durin's Legacy: Corruption and Resurrection">
Even in death, Durin's influence permeated Teyvat. His heart beneath Wyrmrest Valley remained active, and his life energy spread throughout the region. Dvalin, suffering from the poisoned blood he ingested during their battle, fell into a deep slumber, only to be later manipulated by the Abyss Order.

The story of Durin took a new turn with the events involving Albedo and Subject Two. Subject Two, the failed prototype homunculus created by Rhinedottir and left inside Durin, eventually escaped. Driven by envy and hatred for the "successful" Albedo, Subject Two used Durin's blood to create mutated Whopperflowers and attempted to replace Albedo.

<Callout type="success" title="A New Beginning">
The narrative surrounding Durin has evolved significantly, moving from a tale of tragic destruction to one of potential redemption and new life. The creation of Mini Durin and his subsequent humanization represents a profound shift in his destiny.
</Callout>

In recent events (around 2026), Durin's core in Dragonspine became restless, leading to monsters under his influence gaining shapeshifting abilities. Albedo, anticipating a full revival of the original Durin, devised a plan to prevent catastrophe. With the help of Alice and the Hexenzirkel, Albedo drained the remaining life force from Durin's core. He then used this energy, along with materials like the flesh of an immortal monster from the Mare Jivari and Rhinedottir's "embryo," to perform a miracle: transforming Mini Durin into a human.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Humanization of Durin and Future Prospects">
The transformation of Mini Durin into a human marked a pivotal moment in the dragon's lore. This new, human form of Durin, embodying the goodness that Mini Durin possessed, is intended to counteract the original Durin's destructive Abyssal nature. This feat, even rivaling Rhinedottir's creations, was performed by Albedo during the Windblume Festival.

The playable **Pyro** character Durin, now in human form, is described as being in Nod-Krai alongside Albedo, Alice, and his bestie. This new chapter suggests a future where Durin can integrate into society, fulfilling his original, innocent desire to befriend people.
