<Section icon={Swords} title="Durin's Artifact Dilemma: Noblesse Oblige vs. A Day Carved from Rising Winds">
As a 5-star Pyro Sword character in Genshin Impact, Durin offers versatile playstyles, functioning primarily as a Hexerei Pyro Sub-DPS. His kit allows him to switch between Light Form, focusing on consistent Pyro application and RES shredding, and Dark Form, specializing in personal damage through Vaporize or Melt reactions. This duality often leads to a critical question for players: which artifact set, **Noblesse Oblige** or **A Day Carved from Rising Winds**, is truly optimal for Durin? Both sets offer significant benefits, but their strengths align with different aspects of his kit and team roles.

<Callout type="tip" title="Understanding Durin's Forms">
Durin's Elemental Skill allows him to enter two binary forms:
- **Confirmation of Purity (Light Form)**: Activated by Skill > Skill, primarily for consistent Pyro application and RES shred.
- **Denial of Darkness (Dark Form)**: Activated by Skill > Normal Attack, for maximizing personal Vaporize/Melt damage.
</Callout>

The newly introduced **A Day Carved from Rising Winds** set (released in Version 6.3 Luna IV) is designed to synergize perfectly with Durin, especially when paired with Hexerei members. However, **Noblesse Oblige** remains a strong contender due to its universal Elemental Burst support capabilities. Let's delve into a detailed comparison to help you make the best choice for your Durin build.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Noblesse Oblige: The Team Buffer's Choice">
**Noblesse Oblige** is a classic artifact set known for its powerful team-wide buff. For Durin, this set capitalizes on his Burst-oriented playstyle in both forms. The 2-piece bonus provides a flat 20% Elemental Burst DMG increase, directly boosting his primary source of damage. The 4-piece bonus, however, is where its true value lies for team compositions.

<Callout type="info">
Using an Elemental Burst with the 4-piece Noblesse Oblige set increases all party members' ATK by 20% for 12 seconds. This effect is invaluable for teams that rely on multiple characters' damage output.
</Callout>

This set is particularly effective if Durin is primarily used as an off-field support or a sub-DPS whose main contribution is his Elemental Burst and the subsequent team buff. It ensures that not only does Durin deal respectable damage, but he also significantly enhances the damage of his teammates, making him a strong asset in diverse team compositions.

**Noblesse Oblige Breakdown:**

| Set Piece | Effect | Durin's Benefit | Ideal Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2-PC | Elemental Burst DMG +20% | Directly boosts Durin's primary damage source | Sub-DPS, Burst Support |
| 4-PC | Team ATK +20% for 12s after Burst | Enhances entire team's damage, strong support | Off-field Support |

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="A Day Carved from Rising Winds: Maximizing Durin's Personal Damage">
**A Day Carved from Rising Winds** is the newer, more specialized artifact set for Durin. It's generally considered his Best-in-Slot, especially for players aiming to maximize his personal damage, particularly in his Dark Form. This set was introduced with Version 6.3 Luna IV and is designed with Durin's Hexerei synergies in mind.

The 2-piece bonus provides a straightforward 18% ATK increase. The 4-piece bonus is more intricate: after a Normal Attack, Charged Attack, Elemental Skill, or Elemental Burst hits an opponent, Durin gains the "Blessing of Pastoral Winds" effect for 6 seconds, increasing ATK by 25%. If Durin has completed "Witch's Homework" (a Hexerei-related mechanic), this effect upgrades to "Resolve of Pastoral Winds," which also increases his CRIT Rate by an additional 20%. This powerful buff can trigger even when Durin is off-field.

<Callout type="success" title="Hexerei Synergy">
When paired with other Hexerei members, Durin can fully leverage the Resolve of Pastoral Winds effect, gaining both a substantial ATK boost and a critical 20% CRIT Rate increase, significantly enhancing his personal damage.
</Callout>

This set shines when Durin is built for on-field DPS in his Dark Form, or when his personal off-field damage is a priority. The combination of ATK and CRIT Rate buffs makes it superior for raw damage output compared to other options.

**A Day Carved from Rising Winds Breakdown:**

| Set Piece | Effect | Durin's Benefit | Ideal Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2-PC | ATK +18% | General damage increase | On-field DPS, Sub-DPS |
| 4-PC | ATK +25% (up to 20% CRIT Rate with Hexerei) | Massive personal damage boost, especially in Dark Form | On-field DPS, personal damage focused Sub-DPS |

</Section>

Evaluate your entire team's needs before making a final decision.

**Stat Priorities for Both Sets:**

Regardless of the set chosen, Durin's artifact substat priorities remain consistent:
- **Sands**: ATK% (or Elemental Mastery for Vaporize/Melt teams)
- **Goblet**: Pyro DMG Bonus
- **Circlet**: CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG (aim for a 1:2 ratio)

**Substats**: CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, ATK%, Elemental Mastery (especially for Dark Form), Energy Recharge (110-140% if solo Pyro and Skill is not Crowned). Durin generally aims for 2000+ ATK to maximize his A4 passive.

<Section icon={Map} title="Durin's Talent and Playstyle Considerations">
Durin's unique mechanics, particularly his ability to switch between Light and Dark forms, heavily influence artifact choice. His Elemental Burst is central to both forms, making it the highest talent priority.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Elemental Burst">
    Durin's Elemental Burst is his main source of damage and utility. Leveling it up first ensures maximum impact, whether for RES shred in Light Form or raw damage in Dark Form.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Invest in Elemental Skill">
    The Elemental Skill not only dictates his form but also provides Energy restoration. Leveling it up to 10 can significantly reduce his Energy Recharge requirements, potentially freeing up substats for more offensive stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Consider Normal Attack for Dark Form">
    If you plan to play Durin as an on-field DPS in his Dark Form, his Normal Attacks become more potent, especially with C6 Bennett's Pyro infusion. In this specific scenario, investing in Normal Attack talent becomes beneficial.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Energy Management">
If Durin's Elemental Skill is Crowned, you might need little to no Energy Recharge. However, if he's the sole Pyro character in the team, an additional 110%-140% Energy Recharge is recommended to ensure consistent Burst uptime.
</Callout>
